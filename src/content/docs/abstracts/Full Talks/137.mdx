Long-term sustainability of research data services depends strongly on an active and engaged user community. Understanding user needs early on and continuing to involve users as services mature helps ensure that solutions remain relevant across different scientific domains.

Base4NFDI is a joint initiative of all 26 NFDI consortia, including NFDI4Earth, to establish NFDI-wide basic services for FAIR research data management. The project currently coordinates nine basic services and supports their maturation into sustainable offerings, bringing together multiple scientific communities to build interoperable, cross-disciplinary infrastructure. As part of this work, we are developing a comprehensive framework to guide service development from initial concept through to mature service stages.

Recognising the importance of users for sustainability and long-term operation of a RDM service, we have also developed a user engagement component to our framework, comprising all kinds of activities centered around the potential users of a basic service:

**Requirement analysis** is used to systematically capture and structure the needs of different stakeholder groups at an early stage of service development. It typically combines qualitative and quantitative methods such as interviews and surveys to ensure that both explicit and implicit requirements are identified. Base4NFDI support services with tooling, survey design, distribution in NFDI consortia and legal aspects in data protection.

**Persona workshops** are collaborative formats in which representative user archetypes are developed based on empirical observations and community input. These personas help translate diverse user needs into more concrete and relatable profiles, making it easier for development teams to prioritise features. Base4NFDI helps service teams with the introduction of the persona concept in new teams, frameworks for creation of persona archetypes and well developed prompts to bring the personas to life using structured templates.

**Success stories** are short, structured descriptions of functionality from the perspective of the user, typically following an agile development approach. They help to iteratively refine requirements and ensure that development remains focused on user value rather than purely technical considerations. Within Base4NFDI, user stories are used to maintain traceability between community needs and implemented service features across development cycles.

**Training materials** are developed to support onboarding and capacity building for users with varying levels of expertise. These materials include tutorials, documentation, examples, and hands-on guides tailored to different scientific contexts. In Base4NFDI, they are considered essential for lowering barriers to adoption and ensuring that services can be effectively used across disciplines. Especially when created and curated along early service stages, users engage early.

**Incubator projects** provide a structured environment for co-developing and testing early-stage service ideas in close collaboration with NFDI consortia. These projects allow iterative refinement of functionality under realistic conditions before full-scale deployment. Within Base4NFDI, incubators serve as a key mechanism for validating assumptions and accelerating the transition from concept to production-ready service.

**Marketing and outreach** encompass targeted communication strategies, community engagement activities, and dissemination efforts to increase visibility and adoption of services. These activities help build awareness within and across scientific communities and support the formation of sustainable user bases. Service stewards within Base4NFDI help to make Base services well known in new communities when they reach out on conferences, plenaries and workshops in a broad RDM community.

During our talk we would like to present these aspects and show practical examples how services used the engagement to find new users.
