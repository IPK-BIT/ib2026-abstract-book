Micky Lindlar is the Head of Digital Preservation at the TIB in Hannover, where their work addresses one of the most pressing challenges in modern research: the long-term sustainability of digital knowledge. Operating at the critical intersection of data stewardship and archival science, they ensure that complex research datasets, software, and digital objects remain accessible, interpretable, and truly FAIR not just for years, but for decades. As a prominent leader in the international digital preservation community, they are deeply involved in global infrastructure initiatives like the Open Preservation Foundation (OPF). Crucially, their expertise forms a vital bridge between national networks and the European Open Science Cloud (EOSC), where they actively contribute to task forces shaping continental policies for long-term data preservation. Their operational practice and research tackle the complex vulnerabilities of file format obsolescence and the need for robust, machine-actionable metadata. By seamlessly connecting active research data management with long-term digital archiving, Lindlar provides the foundational strategies necessary to build resilient, future-proof infrastructures that align the German NFDI ecosystem with broader EOSC standards.
